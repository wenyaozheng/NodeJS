Hairstyles

Pompadour

A typical pompadour is worn with short sides, with the hair swept upwards and backwards, however as time has passed the look has become more and more versatile and there are a variety of different ways to style a pompadour to make it suit your face shape, lifestyle and career.

Undercut

The undercut is a hairstyle that was fashionable during the Edwardian era, 1920s, 1930s, 1940s, and 2010s predominantly among men. Typically, the hair on the top of the head is long and parted on either the side or center, while the back and sides are buzzed very short.

Crewcut

Ombre

The hair world borrowed the term 'Ombre' from the french word meaning shaded or shading. Ombre hair color is generally darker at the roots through the mid-shaft and then gradually gets lighter from the mid-shaft to the ends.

Wavy

a seemingly permanent beachy look with an effortless texture, youth, and natural malleability that people with other hair types struggle to obtain.

Short

A classic short hairstyle where it is cut above the shoulders in a blunt cut with typically no layers. This style is most common among women.
